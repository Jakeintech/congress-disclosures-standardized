## Governing Law

**5 U.S.C. § 13107 - Custody of and public access to reports**

Source: [5 U.S.C. § 13107 - Legal Information Institute, Cornell Law School](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/13107)

###Statutory Text (Key Excerpt)

> It shall be unlawful for any person to obtain or use a report—
>
> (1) for any unlawful purpose;
>
> (2) for any commercial purpose, other than by news and communications media for dissemination to the general public;
>
> (3) for determining or establishing the credit rating of any individual; or
>
> (4) for use, directly or indirectly, in the solicitation of money for any political, charitable, or other purpose.

## Permitted Uses

This project is designed and intended for:

### 1. Public Transparency & Accountability
- Making congressional financial data more accessible
- Enabling watchdog journalism
- Supporting investigative reporting
- Public oversight of potential conflicts of interest

### 2. News and Communications Media
- Publishing structured datasets for journalists
- Providing analysis and visualization of trading patterns
- Disseminating findings to the general public

### 3. Research and Education
- Academic studies on congressional trading behavior
- Educational projects teaching data engineering
- Open-source development of data processing techniques
- Policy analysis and research

---

## Prohibited Uses

Users of this data **must not**:

### 1. Commercial Gain (except news/media)
- ❌ Do not sell this data as a commercial product
- ❌ Do not use it as primary engine of for-profit trading signal service
- ❌ Do not paywall access unless you are legitimate news organization

### 2. Credit Ratings
- ❌ Do not use disclosure data to compute creditworthiness scores
- ❌ Do not sell credit-related products based on this information

### 3. Fundraising or Solicitation
- ❌ Do not use member contact information for political fundraising
- ❌ Do not use data for charitable solicitation campaigns
- ❌ Do not target members for any form of solicitation

### 4. Unlawful Purposes
- ❌ Do not use data for harassment, doxing, or intimidation
- ❌ Do not violate any other federal, state, or local laws

## Penalties

**5 U.S.C. § 13107(b) - Civil penalty**

> Any person who obtains or uses a report for any purpose prohibited in subsection (a) shall be fined under title 18, United States Code, imprisoned for not more than 1 year, or both.

Violations are **federal criminal offenses** punishable by fines and/or imprisonment.

By using data produced by this pipeline, you agree to:

2. **Not use the data** for any prohibited purpose listed above
3. **Acknowledge the source**: Original reports from disclosures-clerk.house.gov
4. **Accept the disclaimer**: Data may contain extraction errors; verify against original PDFs
